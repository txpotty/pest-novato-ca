Ant Infestations

Ants are a common nuisance, entering properties in search of food and water. While some species are just annoying, others can cause damage or pose health risks. Seeing trails of ants, especially in kitchens or pantries, is a clear sign of an infestation. We identify the type of ant, locate the colony, and apply targeted treatments to eliminate them and prevent re-entry. For more information on our ant control services, visit our Ant Control page.

Ants, small as they may be, can be a significant problem in any home or business. They can contaminate food, create unsanitary conditions, and in some cases, damage structures. Identifying the specific species of ant is crucial for effective treatment, as different types respond to different methods. Our technicians are skilled at distinguishing between common invaders like Argentine ants, odorous house ants, and others, allowing us to apply the most appropriate baits and insecticides. We also focus on sealing entry points and eliminating attractants to prevent future infestations. A proactive approach is key to keeping these persistent pests at bay. Simple steps like keeping food sealed, wiping up spills promptly, and removing trash regularly can make a big difference, but for established colonies, professional intervention is often necessary.

Termite Damage

Termites are silent destroyers, capable of causing significant structural damage to your property before you even realize they are there. Signs of termites include discarded wings, mud tubes on walls, and hollow-sounding wood. Early detection and treatment are vital to prevent costly repairs. Our termite treatment services include thorough inspections and customized treatment plans to eradicate colonies and protect your investment.

Termites are among the most destructive pests a homeowner can face. They feed on cellulose-based materials, which means they can slowly but surely consume the wooden structure of your house. Subterranean termites, prevalent in many areas, build distinctive mud tubes from the ground to their food source. Drywood termites, on the other hand, inhabit the wood itself and can be harder to detect until damage is extensive. Our inspection process for termites involves looking for these signs, as well as using specialized equipment where necessary. Treatment methods can range from liquid termiticides applied to the soil (creating a barrier) to baiting systems that the termites carry back to the colony, effectively eliminating it. Preventative measures, such as removing wood-to-soil contact and ensuring proper drainage, are also crucial in deterring these costly pests. Regular termite inspections, especially in areas prone to infestations, are a wise investment for any property owner.

Rodent Infestations (Mice and Rats)

Mice and rats are not only unsettling to find but also pose serious health risks and can cause damage by gnawing on wires and structures. Signs of rodents include droppings, gnaw marks, and scurrying noises. Our rodent control services include trapping, baiting, exclusion, and sanitation recommendations to remove rodents and prevent their return. We prioritize safety and effectiveness in our rodent management strategies.

Rodents are more than just a nuisance; they can carry diseases, contaminate food, and cause significant damage to property by gnawing on electrical wires, pipes, and structural elements. Mice and rats can squeeze through surprisingly small openings, making it easy for them to enter homes and businesses. Once inside, they can reproduce quickly, leading to a rapidly growing infestation. Our rodent control approach begins with a thorough inspection to identify entry points and harborage areas. We then implement a strategic plan that often involves a combination of trapping, baiting, and exclusion. Exclusion is a critical long-term solution, involving sealing all potential entry points to prevent rodents from getting back in. We also provide advice on sanitation and storage to make your property less attractive to these unwanted guests. Addressing a rodent problem promptly is essential to prevent both health hazards and costly property damage.

Cockroach Problems

Cockroaches are resilient pests known for spreading disease and triggering allergies. Seeing live cockroaches, especially at night, or finding droppings or egg cases indicates an infestation. Cockroach control requires a multi-faceted approach, including targeted treatments and identifying and eliminating sources of food and water. We employ effective strategies to eliminate cockroaches and prevent their return, focusing on sanitation and crack and crevice treatments.

Cockroaches have a reputation for being tough to get rid of, and for good reason. They are highly adaptable and can survive in a variety of environments. Beyond their unpleasant appearance, cockroaches are carriers of bacteria and pathogens that can contaminate food and surfaces, leading to illnesses. They can also exacerbate allergies and asthma, particularly in children. Effective cockroach control involves more than just spraying; it requires identifying the species of cockroach, locating their hiding places and breeding sites, and implementing a comprehensive strategy. This often includes using baits, insect growth regulators, and targeted insecticide applications. Sanitation plays a huge role in preventing cockroach infestations, as they are attracted to food crumbs, spills, and moisture. We work with our clients to identify areas that may be contributing to the problem and provide guidance on how to make their property less hospitable to these persistent pests. A clean environment coupled with professional treatments is the most effective way to achieve long-term cockroach control.

Spider Infestations

While most spiders are harmless, some species can be dangerous, and many people simply have a fear of them. Finding excessive webs or seeing spiders frequently indoors are signs you may need spider removal services. Our treatments focus on reducing the spider population and addressing conditions that attract them, such as other insects. We also focus on removing webs and egg sacs to disrupt their lifecycle.

Spiders are beneficial in that they prey on other insects, but finding them in your home or business can be unnerving for many people. Certain species, like the black widow and brown recluse, are venomous and pose a serious health risk. Even non-venomous spiders can create a messy environment with their webs. Our spider control approach involves identifying the types of spiders present and areas where they are most active. Treatment often includes targeted insecticide applications to common spider habitats, both indoors and outdoors. We also emphasize the importance of reducing the insect population that spiders feed on, as this can significantly decrease the number of spiders on your property. Simple preventative measures like sealing cracks around windows and doors, and regularly removing webs, can also help keep spiders at bay. For persistent spider problems, professional intervention is often the most effective solution.

Bed Bug Extermination

Bed bugs are notorious for causing itchy bites and being very difficult to eliminate without professional help. Finding small, reddish-brown bugs, fecal spots on bedding, or noticing bites when you wake up are strong indicators of a bed bug infestation. Our bed bug extermination services are comprehensive, utilizing a combination of treatments to eradicate these resilient pests from your property, including heat treatments and targeted insecticides.

Bed bugs are a growing problem in many areas, and they are incredibly challenging for a homeowner to eliminate without professional assistance. These small, nocturnal pests feed on blood and can hide in the smallest cracks and crevices, making them extremely difficult to find and eradicate. Signs of a bed bug infestation include itchy bites, particularly in lines or clusters on exposed skin, and the presence of small, reddish-brown stains (fecal spots) on mattresses, bedding, or furniture. Finding live bed bugs or their shed skins are also clear indicators. Our bed bug extermination process is thorough and often involves a combination of methods, including chemical treatments, heat treatments, and vacuuming. Heat treatments are particularly effective as they can penetrate deep into infested areas and kill all stages of the bed bug lifecycle. Preparation for bed bug treatment is crucial, and we provide detailed instructions to our clients to ensure the treatment is as effective as possible. Preventing bed bugs involves being vigilant when traveling and inspecting second-hand furniture before bringing it into your home. If you suspect you have bed bugs, prompt professional intervention is essential to prevent the infestation from spreading.

Flea Treatment

Fleas are a common problem for pet owners, causing discomfort to animals and people alike. Signs of fleas include excessive scratching in pets and small, dark specks (flea dirt) on their fur or bedding. Finding flea bites on ankles and legs is also common. Our flea treatment targets fleas at all stages of their lifecycle, in your home and potentially on your property, to break the cycle and eliminate the infestation.

Fleas may be small, but they can cause a lot of irritation for both pets and their owners. These tiny, jumping insects feed on blood and can reproduce rapidly, leading to a widespread infestation very quickly. While often associated with pets, fleas can also infest homes without animals, particularly if previous residents had pets. Signs of fleas include pets scratching excessively, visible fleas in their fur, or ‘flea dirt’ which looks like small black pepper-like specks. For humans, flea bites are typically small, red, and itchy, often appearing in clusters on the lower legs and ankles. Effective flea control requires treating not just the pet, but also the indoor and sometimes outdoor environment. Our flea treatments utilize products that target adult fleas, as well as their eggs and larvae, to break the lifecycle. Vacuuming thoroughly before treatment is crucial, as it removes eggs, larvae, and pupae and also stimulates adult fleas to emerge from their cocoons, making them more susceptible to treatment. Regular vacuuming after treatment is also recommended. Working with your veterinarian to ensure your pets are on an effective flea preventative is also a key part of a comprehensive flea management plan.

Bee and Wasp Removal

Bees and wasps can pose a threat due to their painful stings, particularly to those with allergies. Discovering a nest on your property, especially near high-traffic areas, is a clear indication that professional bee and wasp removal is necessary. We safely remove nests and take measures to deter these stinging insects from returning, always considering the importance of pollinators like bees when possible.

While many bees are beneficial pollinators, certain species and wasps can be aggressive and pose a stinging hazard, especially when their nests are located close to human activity. Discovering a bee or wasp nest, whether it’s a paper-like wasp nest under eaves or a beehive tucked away in a wall void, can be a cause for concern. Attempting to remove a nest yourself can be dangerous and lead to multiple stings. Our trained technicians have the proper equipment and expertise to safely remove nests and manage populations of stinging insects. For bees, we always assess the situation to determine if relocation is a viable and safe option, particularly for honeybees which are important for pollination. For wasps, which are often more aggressive, eradication is usually necessary. We also identify and address factors that may be attracting stinging insects to your property, such as food sources or accessible nesting sites, to help prevent future problems. Safety is our top priority during bee and wasp removal, ensuring the well-being of both our technicians and your family.

Mosquito Control

Mosquitoes are more than just annoying biters; they can transmit serious diseases like West Nile virus and Zika virus. Reducing mosquito populations is essential for enjoying your outdoor spaces safely. Our mosquito control services focus on identifying and eliminating breeding sites and applying barrier treatments to reduce adult mosquito activity on your property.

Mosquitoes can turn a pleasant evening outdoors into an itchy ordeal. Beyond the annoyance of their bites, they are significant vectors for various diseases, posing a public health concern. Mosquitoes breed in standing water, and even small amounts can be sufficient for them to lay eggs. Our mosquito control strategy involves identifying and treating or eliminating potential breeding sites around your property, such as stagnant water in containers, clogged gutters, or puddles. We also offer barrier treatments, which involve applying a residual insecticide to vegetation and other surfaces where adult mosquitoes rest, creating a protective zone around your yard. Reducing mosquito populations allows you to enjoy your outdoor living spaces without the constant threat of bites and the worry of mosquito-borne illnesses. Simple preventative measures like emptying standing water sources regularly, keeping lawns mowed, and trimming back vegetation can also help reduce mosquito populations on your property. For effective and comprehensive mosquito control, professional service is often the best solution.

Silverfish Control

Silverfish are nocturnal insects that can damage books, paper, clothing, and even food items as they feed on starches and cellulose. Seeing these silvery, fish-shaped insects scurrying away quickly when disturbed is a common sign of their presence. Our silverfish control strategies target their hiding places and sources of moisture, which they require to survive. Addressing humidity issues is often a key component in eliminating silverfish infestations.

Silverfish are ancient insects that can cause damage to a variety of household items. They are attracted to areas with high humidity and prefer dark, undisturbed places like basements, attics, and closets. Their diet consists of materials containing carbohydrates, such as paper, glue, textiles, and even dried foods. Signs of a silverfish infestation include finding the insects themselves, as well as irregular feeding marks on paper or fabric, and small fecal pellets that resemble black pepper. Controlling silverfish involves reducing humidity levels in infested areas through proper ventilation, dehumidifiers, and repairing leaky pipes. We also use targeted treatments to address their hiding spots and disrupt their lifecycle. Removing potential food sources, such as old newspapers and boxes, is also an important preventative measure. While not harmful to humans in terms of bites or disease transmission, silverfish can cause significant damage to valuable belongings, making professional control a necessary step to protect your property.

Tick Control

Ticks are parasites that can transmit diseases to both humans and pets, including Lyme disease. If you spend time outdoors, particularly in wooded or grassy areas, you may be at risk of encountering ticks. Finding ticks on yourself or your pets after being outdoors is a clear sign of their presence in your environment. Our tick control services focus on treating outdoor areas where ticks are likely to be found, such as tall grasses, brush, and leaf litter around your property perimeter.

Ticks are a significant concern due to their ability to transmit various diseases, making them more than just a pesky insect. They are commonly found in wooded areas, tall grass, and brush, and they attach to hosts (humans or animals) as they brush past. Preventing tick bites starts with taking precautions when spending time in tick-prone areas, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using tick repellents. However, if you have a tick problem on your property, professional intervention can help reduce the risk of encountering them. Our tick control strategies often involve treating areas of your yard where ticks are likely to hide and breed. This can include targeting tall grasses, overgrown vegetation, and leaf litter along property lines and pathways. We can also provide advice on landscaping and property maintenance practices that can make your yard less hospitable to ticks, such as keeping lawns mowed and removing leaf litter. Reducing the tick population on your property is an important step in protecting yourself and your family from tick-borne illnesses.

Moth Control

Certain types of moths can cause damage to clothing, carpets, and stored food items. Seeing moths flying around your home, finding larvae or pupae, or discovering damage to fabrics or pantry items are signs you may need moth control. Our services target the specific type of moth infestation and include treatments for infested areas as well as recommendations for prevention.

While some moths are harmless and even beautiful, certain species, such as clothes moths and pantry moths, can be destructive pests in a home. Clothes moths feed on natural fibers like wool, silk, and cotton, causing irreversible damage to clothing, carpets, and upholstery. Pantry moths infest stored food products like grains, cereals, and dried fruits, contaminating them and making them unusable. Signs of a clothes moth infestation include finding small, irregular holes in fabrics, silken tubes or cases where larvae live, and potentially the moths themselves. Pantry moth infestations are often indicated by finding moths flying around food storage areas, webbing in food containers, or larvae. Our moth control services begin with identifying the type of moth and locating the source of the infestation. Treatment involves targeting the larvae and adult moths in infested areas. For clothes moths, this may include treating closets and storage areas. For pantry moths, it involves identifying and discarding infested food items and treating pantry shelves. We also provide advice on proper storage of clothing and food to prevent future infestations and keep your belongings and pantry items safe.
